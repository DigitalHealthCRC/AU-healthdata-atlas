AU Health Data Atlas

Welcome to the AU Health Data Atlas

The Atlas maps Australia's health data landscape: the custodians that hold health datasets, how researchers can apply for access, and how custodians connect to one another through data flows and linkage arrangements.

What you can do here

  • Network — an interactive map of data custodians, coloured by jurisdiction and sized by their dataset holdings. Hover to highlight a custodian's connections; click a node for its full profile, including governance, timelines and contacts.
  • Pathways — each custodian's data access process drawn as a metro-style line across three lanes: what the researcher does, what ethics & regulatory bodies require, and what the custodian handles.
  • Datasets — a searchable catalogue of the datasets each custodian holds, with flags for whether data is identifiable and linkable.

Where the data comes from

Content is hand-curated by the Digital Health CRC from publicly available custodian sources — websites, application portals, governance documents and published data dictionaries — and compiled into a structured pathway register. That register is parsed into a knowledge graph, cross-checked (connections between custodians are matched and manually reviewed before they appear as links), and exported as the static bundle this page reads. Every custodian profile lists the source URLs it was built from.

This is a navigational guide, not an authoritative register. Access requirements, timelines and contacts change — always verify details with the custodian before relying on them.
